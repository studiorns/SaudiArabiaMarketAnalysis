Market Insights

Key Takeaway

The Saudi Arabia market shows a distinct summer seasonality with peaks in June and July. The market has a very short 24-day booking window, suggesting a more spontaneous travel pattern compared to other markets.

Seasonal Pattern

The Saudi Arabia market demonstrates a distinct seasonal pattern with hotel guests peaking in July (29,809) and June (20,401), with August (22,492) also showing strong performance. For travel queries, the highest peak occurs in July (25.0065), followed by June (24.1228) and April (21.0106). The lowest performance for both metrics occurs during the winter months (November-February).

Seasonal Distribution

  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 21% of annual travel queries
  • Spring (Mar-May): 22% of annual travel queries
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 34% of annual travel queries
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): 23% of annual travel queries

Peak Performance

  • Top Month (Travel Queries): July (25.01)
  • Top Month (Hotel Guests): July (29,809)
  • Top Month (Flight Searches): January (14,184)
  • Secondary Peak: June (24.12) & April (21.01)

Implications for Marketing

The distinct summer seasonality of the Saudi Arabia market suggests that marketing efforts should be concentrated in Q2 to capture the peak travel period. The extremely short 24-day average booking window indicates that campaigns should be launched approximately 3-4 weeks before the target travel period, with a focus on immediate conversion rather than long-term awareness building.

Strategic Opportunity

The winter months (December-February) represent an opportunity for growth, as they currently account for only 21% of annual travel queries. Targeted winter campaigns highlighting Abu Dhabi's pleasant winter climate, outdoor activities, and cultural offerings could help balance the seasonal distribution.

2025 Forecast Insight

The 2025 forecast shows a relatively flat growth of 1.0% in the moderate scenario, with some months showing negative growth compared to 2024. This suggests a maturing market that requires innovative marketing approaches to stimulate further growth.

Key Takeaway

The Saudi Arabia market shows positive growth in brand health metrics but declining travel queries in the second half of 2024. This suggests increasing competition from alternative destinations despite strong awareness and consideration.

Market Performance

The Saudi Arabia market has shown mixed performance in 2024. Brand health metrics are strong, with awareness at 95.40% (+2.40pp vs Q4'23), consideration at 61.47% (+1.54pp vs Q4'23), and intent at 46.30% (+0.56pp vs Q4'23). However, travel queries declined in the second half of 2024, with July (-17.27% YoY), August (-8.84% YoY), November (-12.89% YoY), and December (-11.86% YoY) all showing negative growth.

Positive Trends

  • Awareness: Strong growth to 95.40% (+2.40pp)
  • Consideration: Solid increase to 61.47% (+1.54pp)
  • Hotel Guests: Strong growth in Q2 and Q3
  • Flight Searches: January 2024 showed 106.49% YoY growth

Negative Trends

  • Travel Queries: Decline in H2 2024 (Jul-Dec)
  • Growth Forecast: Only 1.0% YoY growth projected
  • Flight Searches: Q3 2024 showed decline in August (-22.77%)
  • Media Efficiency: Declining impact of impressions on queries

Market Challenges

Despite strong brand health metrics, the Saudi Arabia market faces challenges in converting awareness and consideration into actual travel queries and bookings. The modest 1.0% growth forecast for 2025 suggests a maturing market that requires innovative marketing approaches to stimulate further growth.

Market Challenge

The Saudi Arabia market shows a concerning trend of declining travel queries in the second half of 2024 despite strong brand health metrics. This suggests that while Abu Dhabi is well-known and considered by Saudi travelers, there are increasing barriers to conversion that need to be addressed through targeted marketing strategies.

Advanced Recommendation

Implement a dynamic media allocation model that adjusts spend based on the 1-month lag effect, with heavier investment in May to impact the June-July peak. Focus on addressing the top barriers of "already visited" and "not value for money" by highlighting new attractions and experiences, and emphasizing the unique value proposition compared to competing destinations.

Key Takeaway

Strategic recommendations for the Saudi Arabia market focus on addressing the "already visited" barrier, improving value perception, highlighting new attractions, promoting family-friendly activities, and developing winter-specific campaigns.

Repeat Visitation Strategy

Develop a "What's New in Abu Dhabi" campaign to counter the high "already visited" barrier (50%).

  • Create content highlighting new attractions, experiences, and developments
  • Develop a loyalty program with special benefits for repeat visitors
  • Partner with Saudi travel agencies to create "What's New" tour packages
  • Implement a social media campaign featuring new experiences

Projected outcome: Increase repeat visitation by 15-20%

Value Perception Enhancement

Launch a "More for Your Money" campaign to address the significant increase in "not value for money" perception (+14pp).

  • Create bundled packages with clear value propositions
  • Highlight complimentary experiences and services
  • Develop exclusive offers for Saudi travelers
  • Create content comparing value with competing destinations

Projected outcome: Reduce "not value for money" perception by 8-10pp

Family Experience Enhancement

Develop a comprehensive "Family Adventures in Abu Dhabi" campaign to address the increase in "not much entertainment for children" perception (+18pp).

  • Create content featuring family-friendly attractions and activities
  • Develop special family packages with children's activities included
  • Partner with Saudi family influencers to showcase experiences
  • Implement targeted social media campaigns for family decision-makers

Projected outcome: Reduce "not much entertainment for children" perception by 10-12pp

Winter Campaign Development

Launch a "Perfect Winter Escape" campaign to boost travel during the low season (December-February).

  • Highlight Abu Dhabi's pleasant winter climate compared to other destinations
  • Create content showcasing outdoor winter activities
  • Develop special winter packages with value-added components
  • Implement targeted promotions for the short booking window (24 days)

Projected outcome: Increase winter travel queries by 15-20%
